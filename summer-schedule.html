Daily Routine (8am – 3pm)

The daily flow with Emma. Adjust times based on activity days.

8:00 AM

Arrival & Breakfast Wrap-Up

Emma arrives. Girls finish breakfast, get dressed, sunscreen. Quick chat about the day’s plan.

8:30 – 9:00 AM

Morning Learning Block

Phonics flash cards, reading practice, or workbook time (Tues & Thurs). Other days: free play / art / puzzles while Emma preps for the outing.

9:00 – 11:30 AM

Main Activity

This is the big block — park day, museum trip, swimming, science kit, climbing class, or whatever’s on the weekly plan.

11:30 AM – 12:15 PM

Lunch

Out on adventure days (Monday). Home lunch on other days. Keep it simple — sandwiches, fruit, snacks.

12:15 – 1:00 PM

Quiet Time / Rest

Wind down after lunch. Audiobook, coloring, calm play. Recharge for the afternoon.

1:00 – 2:30 PM

Afternoon Activity

Pool / water play on swim days. Reading & learning on academic days. Free play, crafts, or backyard time on other days.

2:30 – 3:00 PM

Clean Up & Wrap

Tidy up, pack away supplies, quick snack. Emma wraps up for the day.

Sign-Up Activities

These need registration, booking, or sign-up ahead of time. Research and lock these in early — summer spots fill fast.

Triangle Rock Club

Raleigh & Morrisville • Ages 5+
Youth climbing programs, “First Ascents” beginner classes, and summer camps. Two locations in the Triangle. Great for a 5-6 year old first-timer.

Dead Broke Farm

Raleigh • Ages 3+ • 110 acres
Private lessons, pony rides, and summer horseback riding camps for ages 5+. No prior experience needed. Real farm setting, not a ring. She’ll love it.
Book Now — Fills Fast

deadbrokefarm.com →

Gymcarolina Gymnastics

Raleigh • Ages 3+
Preschool & intro classes, plus full summer camps (ages 5+). Great structure for active kids. Multiple class times available.

The Little Gym

North Raleigh / Wake Forest • Ages 3-10
Summer camps with gymnastics, active play, and age-appropriate skill building. Fun-first approach, good for a kid who hasn’t done structured gym before.

thelittlegym.com →

Flour Power Kids Cooking Studios

North Hills & Falls River • Ages 5-8
“Lil’ Chef” summer camps and individual classes. Hands-on cooking, kitchen safety, themed sessions. Pick 4, 6, or 8 classes and use on your schedule.

Pullen Arts Center

Raleigh (Pullen Park area) • Ages 5+
City of Raleigh art classes: pottery, painting, drawing, printmaking, jewelry, bookmaking. Summer workshops available. Right next to Pullen Park.

Outdoor Spots & Day Trips

No sign-up needed. Rotate through these on Mondays or whenever the weather’s good.

Free

Dorothea Dix Park

Wide open fields, great skyline views, perfect for running around and picnics.

Free

Pullen Park

Paddle boats, train ride, playground, carousel. Classic Raleigh kid spot. Small fees for rides.

Free

NCMA Park & Trails

Art-filled nature trails, wooden boardwalks, outdoor sculptures. Easy walking for little legs.

Free

Durant Nature Preserve

“Secret” creek trails, fishing, natural play areas, fairy house building. North Raleigh gem.

Free

Annie Louise Wilkerson Preserve

Rocky outcrops, short kid-friendly trails, nature exploration. Feels like a mini adventure.

Free

Lake Johnson Park

Paved trails great for little walkers. Beautiful lake views. Easy and chill.

Free

Hemlock Bluffs (Cary)

Wooden boardwalks through cool hemlock forest. Nature center on-site. Educational and pretty.

Free

Laurel Hills Park

Playground, open fields, shaded trails. Solid neighborhood park for low-key days.

NC Museum of Natural Sciences

Downtown Raleigh. Free general admission. Dinosaurs, live animals, nature exhibits. Huge hit with kids.

Museum of Life & Science

Durham. Outdoor science park, splash pad, butterfly house. Worth the drive. Paid admission.

Marbles Kids Museum

Downtown Raleigh. Interactive exhibits built for this age. IMAX theater too. Paid admission.

Fantasy Lake

Water park vibes. Good for a full morning outing on hot days.
